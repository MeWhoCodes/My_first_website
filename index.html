4. The Great King Shibi

Once there lived a king, Shibi. He was just and powerful but the only problem was that he was foolish. One day his minister
informed him that a poor woman and her child were dying of hunger. The king decided to visit the woman, upon reaching he saw
that the woman was sleeping and her child was eating mud. “Eating mud? Shibi wants to know why this child is eating mud!” said
the great Shibi. One of the ministers replied, “King Shibi, the child has nothing to eat, he is starving sire!” King Shibi was
as foolish as great he was, therefore he didn’t knew what starving was. “Minister! What is starving?”asked Shibi to one of his
minister, “O great Shibi, starving is the most wonderful thing that can happen to oneself “ said the minister in sarcasm. But
Shibi didn’t knew what  sarcasm was, for he was as foolish as great he was. “Oh! Even a poor child is starving while I am not
able to feel this pleasure of so-called ‘starving’, I think I will also starve” he muttered. “Minister! How do we starve” he
enquired. One of the ministers replied “Sir, to starve, we should first donate all our property to the poor…” “Ministers,
distribute all my property to the poor, now!” ordered Shibi. “...then” continued the minister, “we have to donate our regular
clothes and wear ragged clothes…” suddenly the king started to undress and gave all his clothes to the poor son and his mother
and instead wore the shawl the child was wearing. “Then…” gulped the minister fearing the king would kill him, “you have to step
down the thorn and make a new king”. Shibi almost immediately, without a second thought announced, “I Shibi, make this poor
child and his poor mother the next ruler of this kingdom”. The greedy ministers all became happy that they would be able to
share the treasure the king had donated. But suddenly, the earth shook and lord vishnu came out and in one step crushed all the
greedy ministers for trying to inflict pain on king Shibi, then he said “King Shibi, you shall live forever in my eternal place
and this kingdom shall flourish”. The poor son and the poor woman ruled the kingdom henceforth and king Shibi lived peacefully
in Vishnu’s abode, for he was as kind hearted as foolish he was.

5. On That Dark Night

It was a dark and gloomy night, I was returning from my office. The road lights were flickering and there was nobody on the
road. It was very late for traveling alone. Although I was in a jolly mood, as it was my birthday, I was getting an uncanny
feeling as if something was not right. I noticed that a group of people were  following me, they had black masks, goggles, they
even wore black robes. They walked relatively faster than me, so eventually they were walking beside me. I watched them from the
corner of my eye so that they couldn't notice me seeing them. Their presumed leader, who was leading the group, had something in
his pocket, something heavy. I was horror struck, it was a gun, the leader was carrying a gun. I leapt on the leader, at least I
could  tackle  him, but it seemed to me as if he was ready for the move, he took the gun out and fired. Two loud bangs were
heard and I fell on the tarmac with a “thud”. I couldn’t feel my body chest-down, two of the black-robed-men grabbed me from the
collar and dragged me to the nearest bench, their leader instructed them to leave me alone. I struggled to stand but stumbled on
the bench and sat on it. The leader came near me and took off his mask. “ Oh..no it can’t be you...no it can’t be..”I looked
down and saw there were no wounds!, except a few scratches from the humiliating fall. “Happy birthday brother” he said. After
all, it was one of my brother’s pranks, he always likes to humiliate me.
